Mission & Vision of Club

Holistic Personality Development: We are dedicated to providing a comprehensive platform for holistic personality development, encompassing communication skills, emotional intelligence, leadership abilities, and self-awareness.

Promoting Inclusivity: We are committed to promoting inclusivity and creating a safe and welcoming environment for all students, ensuring that everyone has an equal opportunity to enhance their personality.

A Hub of Personal Development Excellence: Proficio Persona Club aspires to be recognized as a hub of excellence in personal development, setting the standard for similar clubs and organizations in universities nationwide.

Global Citizens: We envision our members as global citizens, possessing not only the necessary academic knowledge but also the interpersonal and life skills required to excel in a diverse and interconnected world.

Corporate Insights

Proficio Persona Club, GLA University Organized "Corporate Insights" seminar on 1st November 2023, featuring renowned psychologist Dr. Nelu, CEO, and founder of Mentorick, was a resounding success. The event commenced with an inspirational address by Dr. Ritesh Sharma, setting the stage for Dr. Nelu's insightful presentation. The event was graced by the esteemed presence of Prof. Devika Agarwal and Dr. Hariprapan Sharma, with more than 300 spectators in attendance. Dr. Nelu captivated the audience by shedding light on the challenges faced in the corporate world, including time mismanagement, stress, lack of confidence, and language barriers. She provided expert guidance and shared her experiences with grace and precision, encouraging attendees to reflect on these issues and discover solutions.

The Eloquent Presenters

Proficio Persona Club, the personality development club of the Institute of Business Management organised an event called "The Eloquent Presenters". It took place on 7th January 2022 in the conference hall AB-5. It was organised with the aim to develop self-confidence among students and remove their stage fear. During the event, students were given random topics and were asked to extempore on the topic. More than 80 students participated to improve their communication and public speaking skills. There were two rounds, in the first-round students were given random topics and some students were shortlisted out of them for the second round. In the second round, students were asked to extempore a common topic.
